Dengue Information

Dengue is a mosquito-borne viral disease commonly seen in tropical and subtropical regions. It causes sudden high fever, severe body pain, headache, nausea, vomiting, and skin rashes. Most cases are mild, but severe dengue can become life-threatening if not treated immediately.

Symptoms

  • High Fever – sudden onset
  • Severe Headache – especially behind the eyes
  • Body Pain – muscle & joint pain (“break-bone fever”)
  • Skin Rash
  • Nausea, Vomiting, Fatigue

Severe Dengue – Warning Signs

Seek immediate hospital care if you notice:

  • Severe stomach pain
  • Continuous vomiting
  • Bleeding from gums or nose
  • Blood in vomit
  • Sudden drop in blood pressure

Prevention

  • Use mosquito repellent regularly
  • Wear long-sleeved clothing
  • Use mosquito nets while sleeping
  • Remove stagnant water around your house
  • Keep surroundings clean

Treatment

  • Mild Dengue: Rest, hydration, paracetamol (avoid aspirin/ibuprofen)
  • Severe Dengue: Requires hospital admission, IV fluids, monitoring
